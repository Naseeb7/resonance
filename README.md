# Resonance

Resonance is a premium mobile-first speech therapy PWA prototype.

It is designed as a calm daily articulation practice experience centered on emotional safety, supportive progression, and confidence-building repetition.

**Quick Access**

- Live Demo: `https://your-demo-url.com`
- GitHub Repository: `https://github.com/your-org/resonance`

## Design Philosophy

Resonance borrows the strongest idea from Duolingo-style lesson systems: short, structured progression that makes daily practice feel achievable.

What changes for speech therapy is the emotional context. Articulation practice can feel vulnerable, so the interaction model avoids punitive feedback and high-pressure pacing. The product prioritizes:

- calmer progression over urgency
- supportive feedback over correctness policing
- confidence-building repetition over performance framing
- emotionally safe UX over gamified intensity

## Lesson Experience

The daily flow is intentionally simple:

1. Welcome
2. Listen & Repeat
3. Word Recognition
4. Mirror Mode
5. Reflection
6. Completion

Each step is brief, focused, and designed to sustain momentum without overwhelm.

## Features

- Mobile-first installable PWA shell
- Smooth, guided lesson progression
- Supportive non-punitive feedback language
- Listen & Repeat articulation practice
- Word Recognition listening calibration
- Mirror Mode articulation awareness guidance
- Reflection check-in for emotional pacing
- Calm completion with mature streak/progress framing

## Innovation: Mirror Mode

Speech therapy is physical. Progress depends on awareness of mouth shape, airflow, jaw tension, and pacing, not just abstract scoring.

Mirror Mode supports that embodiment directly: front-camera guidance, subtle framing cues, and calm rhythm support. It is intentionally not a fake AI evaluator. The goal is self-awareness and articulation confidence through guided observation.

## Interaction & Motion Design

Motion and interaction are designed to feel clinically calm and premium:

- soft screen transitions and restrained timing
- tactile mobile-first press/selection states
- subtle depth and progress cues
- emotionally supportive feedback transitions

The experience aims to feel trustworthy, focused, and app-like on mobile browsers.
